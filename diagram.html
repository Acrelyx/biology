Anatomy

The table below details the simple yet strikingly effective physiological features of the horseshoe crab.

Feature Function
Body Carapace Protection of internal organs
Legs/Chelicerae/Pedipalps Locomotion, feeding (guiding/crushing food), male grasping during mating
Book Gills Respiratory gas exchange, occasional swimming
Compound Eyes Vision, mate finding, adaptation to day/night light sensitivity
Median/Endoparietal/Ventral Eyes Detection of visible and UV light, lunar cycle tracking (important for spawning)
Chemoreceptors "Smelling" or detecting prey
Digestive System (Gizzard) Crushing and grinding food (lacking jaws)
Circulatory System (Blue Blood/Hemocyanin) Oxygen transport throughout the body
Amebocytes/LAL Pathogen detection, rapid blood clotting, basis for biomedical testing (Limulus Amebocyte Lysate)
Innate Immune System Robust defence against invading pathogens (coagulation, agglutination, antibacterial substances)
Exoskeleton/Molting Growth (shedding old shell to increase size)
Telson (Tail) Self-righting mechanism if overturned, protection
